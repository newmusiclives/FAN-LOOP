Social Media Launch Toolkit

Everything you need to launch your music across Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter/X. Copy-ready scripts, templates, and strategies for a full 30-day campaign.

30-Day Content Calendar 30 Days

▼

A complete day-by-day posting plan rotating across Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter/X. Each day includes the platform, content type, and a brief description of what to post.

Day 1
Instagram Reel
Teaser clip: 15-second snippet of the hook of your new song with text overlay "Something new is coming" and a countdown sticker.
Day 2
TikTok Video
Behind-the-scenes studio footage showing the moment you finished recording. Raw and authentic, no polish needed. Caption teases the release.
Day 3
Twitter/X Thread
4-tweet thread telling the story behind why you wrote this song. Share the emotional origin, the creative process, and end with a teaser link.
Day 4
Instagram Story
Poll story: "What vibe are you feeling today?" with options that relate to your song's mood. Follow up with a snippet tied to the winning answer.
Day 5
TikTok Video
Transition video: Start in casual clothes, snap transition to performance mode with your song playing. Use trending transition format.
Day 6
Twitter/X Tweet
Single tweet with cover art reveal. "Here it is. [SONG TITLE] drops [DATE]. This one is personal." Pin this tweet to your profile.
Day 7
Instagram Carousel
5-slide carousel: Slide 1 cover art, Slides 2-4 handwritten lyrics from key verses, Slide 5 release date announcement with pre-save link in bio CTA.
Day 8
TikTok Video
Lyric breakdown: Pick the most emotional lyric, display it on screen, and explain what it means to you in 30 seconds. Genuine and unscripted feel.
Day 9
Twitter/X Poll
Engagement poll: "What matters most to you in a song?" with options like Lyrics, Beat, Vibe, Vocals. Reply to voters with how your song delivers on their pick.
Day 10
Instagram Reel
Acoustic or stripped-back version of the chorus. Sit with a guitar or at a piano. Intimate setting, good lighting. CTA: "Full version drops soon."
Day 11
TikTok Video
Challenge launch: Create a simple dance move or gesture tied to your chorus. Do it yourself first. Use text: "Can you do this? Show me." with your song as the sound.
Day 12
Twitter/X Thread
Production thread: 5 tweets breaking down how the beat was made, what instruments were used, who produced it, and what inspired the sonic direction.
Day 13
Instagram Story
Countdown story with the official countdown sticker set to release day. Add a question box: "What are you hoping this song sounds like?"
Day 14
TikTok Video
React to your own song: Film yourself hearing the final master for the first time. Genuine reaction content performs extremely well on TikTok.
Day 15
Instagram Reel
Release day announcement Reel. High energy, fast cuts of you in the studio, cover art, streaming platforms. Text overlay: "OUT NOW" with link in bio CTA.
Day 16
TikTok Video
Release day TikTok: "I just dropped my new song and I need you to hear it." Play the best 15 seconds. Point to the link in bio. High energy delivery.
Day 17
Twitter/X Thread
Release day thread: Tweet 1 announces the drop with link. Tweets 2-4 share lyrics, behind-the-scenes photos. Tweet 5 asks fans to quote-tweet with their reaction.
Day 18
Instagram Story
Share every fan reaction, DM, and comment as a story repost. Add "Thank you" stickers and heart emojis. Show genuine gratitude for early listeners.
Day 19
TikTok Video
Duet-ready content: Post a clip specifically designed for duets. Sing the chorus and leave space for others to harmonize, react, or add their take.
Day 20
Twitter/X Tweet
Milestone celebration tweet: "We hit [NUMBER] streams in the first week. I started this in my bedroom. Thank you." Authentic gratitude with a screenshot of stats.
Day 21
Instagram Reel
Fan compilation Reel: Stitch together clips of fans singing along, screenshots of comments, and streaming stats. Set it to your song. Community celebration.
Day 22
TikTok Video
Storytime format: "The real story behind [SONG TITLE]" with you talking directly to camera. Vulnerable, honest, let people connect to the meaning.
Day 23
Twitter/X Thread
Lessons learned thread: "5 things I learned releasing [SONG TITLE]" covering what surprised you, what worked, what you would do differently. Value-driven content.
Day 24
Instagram Carousel
Lyric art carousel: Each slide is a beautifully designed lyric quote from the song. Shareable, saveable content. Last slide has streaming link CTA.
Day 25
TikTok Video
Use a trending sound or format but remix it to promote your song. Ride the algorithm wave by pairing trending content with your music promotion.
Day 26
Twitter/X Tweet
Direct ask tweet: "If [SONG TITLE] has been on repeat for you, drop a quote tweet and tell me your favorite line. I read every single one." Engagement driver.
Day 27
Instagram Story
Interactive quiz story: "How well do you know [SONG TITLE]?" with quiz stickers about lyrics, the inspiration, and fun facts about the recording process.
Day 28
TikTok Video
Live snippet or concert-style performance of the song. Even if filmed in your room, set up lighting and perform like you are on stage. Energy matters.
Day 29
Instagram Reel
Thank you Reel: Montage of the entire 30-day journey. Studio clips, release day, fan reactions, milestones. Text: "This is just the beginning."
Day 30
Twitter/X Thread
Wrap-up thread: Share final stats, favorite fan moments, biggest surprises, and tease what is coming next. End with a heartfelt thank you to everyone who listened.

Posting Best Practices Guide

▼

Optimal posting times, frequency recommendations, and platform-specific strategies to maximize reach and engagement.

Instagram - Optimal Posting Times

  • Monday: 11 AM, 1 PM, 5 PM
  • Tuesday: 9 AM, 1 PM, 5 PM
  • Wednesday: 11 AM, 1 PM, 3 PM
  • Thursday: 11 AM, 12 PM, 5 PM
  • Friday: 10 AM, 12 PM, 3 PM
  • Saturday: 9 AM, 11 AM, 1 PM
  • Sunday: 10 AM, 1 PM, 4 PM
  • Peak Day: Wednesday consistently shows highest engagement for music content
  • Time Zone: All times in your audience's primary time zone. Check Instagram Insights to confirm.

Instagram - Frequency Guide

  • Feed Posts (Reels/Carousels): 3-5 times per week
  • Stories: 5-10 per day during launch week, 3-5 per day ongoing
  • Reels: At least 3 per week, Instagram heavily favors Reels in discovery
  • Carousels: 1-2 per week, great for saves and shares
  • Static Photos: 1 per week maximum, these get the lowest reach
  • Lives: 1-2 per week during launch period for real-time connection
  • Key Rule: Never post more than once every 4 hours to avoid suppressing your own reach

TikTok - Optimal Posting Times

  • Monday: 6 AM, 10 AM, 10 PM
  • Tuesday: 2 AM, 4 AM, 9 AM
  • Wednesday: 7 AM, 8 AM, 11 PM
  • Thursday: 9 AM, 12 PM, 7 PM
  • Friday: 5 AM, 1 PM, 3 PM
  • Saturday: 11 AM, 7 PM, 8 PM
  • Sunday: 7 AM, 8 AM, 4 PM
  • Peak Day: Tuesday and Thursday see the highest engagement rates on TikTok
  • Note: TikTok's algorithm is less time-dependent than Instagram. Content quality matters more than posting time.

TikTok - Frequency Guide

  • Videos: 1-3 per day during launch week, 1 per day ongoing
  • Minimum: 4 videos per week to stay in algorithmic favor
  • Maximum: 3 per day, more than that dilutes engagement across posts
  • Video Length: 15-60 seconds performs best. Under 15 sec for hooks, 30-60 sec for stories.
  • Consistency Rule: Post at the same times daily so the algorithm learns your schedule
  • Reply Videos: Make 2-3 reply videos per week responding to comments. These get boosted.
  • Key Rule: The first 3 seconds determine if someone watches. Invest 80% of your effort in the hook.

Twitter/X - Optimal Posting Times

  • Monday: 8 AM, 11 AM, 4 PM
  • Tuesday: 8 AM, 10 AM, 2 PM
  • Wednesday: 9 AM, 12 PM, 5 PM
  • Thursday: 8 AM, 11 AM, 4 PM
  • Friday: 9 AM, 11 AM, 1 PM
  • Saturday: 10 AM, 12 PM
  • Sunday: 10 AM, 2 PM
  • Peak Day: Wednesday and Thursday have the highest engagement on Twitter/X
  • Note: Tweets have a much shorter lifespan than other platforms. Peak engagement happens within the first 30 minutes.

Twitter/X - Frequency Guide

  • Tweets: 3-5 per day during launch, 2-3 per day ongoing
  • Threads: 2-3 per week, threads get significantly more impressions than single tweets
  • Quote Tweets: 1-2 per day engaging with fans, other artists, or relevant content
  • Replies: Reply to every mention during launch week. Engagement breeds engagement.
  • Spaces: Host 1 Twitter Space per week during launch for live Q&A
  • Key Rule: Twitter rewards conversation. Do not just broadcast. Reply, quote, engage. The algorithm pushes people who are actively talking.
Cross-Platform Strategy Rules
1. Do Not Cross-Post Identical Content Each platform has different cultures and formats. A TikTok reposted to Instagram Reels with the TikTok watermark gets suppressed by Instagram's algorithm. Refilm or re-export natively for each platform. 2. Content Ratio: 80/20 Rule 80% of your content should be valuable, entertaining, or emotionally resonant without directly selling anything. 20% can be direct promotion like "Go stream my song." If every post is "Link in bio," people will unfollow. 3. The 30-Minute Engagement Window After posting on any platform, spend the next 30 minutes actively engaging. Reply to comments, like posts in your niche, respond to stories. This signals to the algorithm that you are an active user and boosts your post's distribution. 4. Hashtag Strategy by Platform Instagram: Use 8-15 hashtags. Mix broad (500K+ posts) with niche (10K-100K posts). Put them in the caption, not the comments. TikTok: Use 3-5 hashtags. One broad, two niche, one trending if relevant. Keep them in the caption. Twitter/X: Use 1-2 hashtags maximum. More than that looks spammy and actually decreases engagement on Twitter. 5. Release Day Posting Sequence Post in this order on release day: - 12:00 AM: Twitter/X announcement tweet with link - 6:00 AM: Instagram Story countdown completion celebration - 8:00 AM: Instagram Reel with the strongest 15 seconds of the song - 10:00 AM: TikTok video with hook and CTA - 12:00 PM: Twitter/X thread telling the story behind the song - 2:00 PM: Instagram carousel with lyrics and streaming links - 4:00 PM: TikTok second video, different angle or format - 6:00 PM: Instagram Story reposting fan reactions - 8:00 PM: Twitter/X engagement push, quote-tweeting fan reactions - 10:00 PM: TikTok late-night post, more personal or vulnerable tone 6. The Content Recycling Rule A TikTok script can become a Twitter thread. A Twitter thread can become an Instagram carousel. An Instagram Reel concept can become a TikTok video. Adapt the content to the platform's format and culture, but the core ideas can and should be reused across all three. 7. Batching and Scheduling Film all your content for the week in one session. Spend one day filming 5-7 TikToks, shooting 3-4 Reels, and writing all your tweets. Then schedule them using tools like Later, Buffer, or Hootsuite. This prevents burnout and keeps your posting consistent even on days when you do not feel creative. 8. Analytics Check-In Schedule Review your analytics every Sunday evening. Look at which posts got the most engagement, which times performed best, and where your followers are located. Adjust your posting schedule for the next week based on what the data tells you, not what a guide tells you. Your audience is unique.
